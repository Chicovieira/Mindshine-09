Finger Reader

Finger Reader is a finger device that tells the user what they are pointing to. The FingerReader is a wearable augmentation that consists of a camera worn on the finger, vision algorithms and speech I/O that allows the user to simply point at something in order to perform a task of recognition and interpretation of what the camera sees and hears establishing, spoken result for him or her. For example, it can help users toread printed text on the go.

The text pointed at with the tip of the index finger is recognized and translated orally. To aid text navigation and explore documents efficiently, it provides guidance through haptic and audio feedback.

DOSVOX

DOSVOX is a system for microcomputers of the PC line that communicates with the user through voice synthesis, thus enabling the use of computers by the visually impaired, who acquire, thus a high degree of independence in study and work.

The system communicates with the visually impaired through voice synthesis in Portuguese, and the text synthesis can be configured for other languages.

DOSVOX establishes a friendly dialogue, through specific programs and adaptive interfaces. This makes it unbeatable in terms of quality and easiness, consequently taking the computer to the comfortable means of communication and possibly friendly.

MECDaisy

MECDaisy is a software developed by UFRJ that allows reading / listening to books in Daisy format. The Daisy format – Digital Accessible Information System – is a document scanning standard used for the production of accessible books.

Unlike audio-books, MECDaisy allows facilitated navigation through the books and greater interaction at the time of reading, enabling the localization of terms and words, agile navigation through the book's index, inclusion of notes, all through guidelines verbalized by the system itself.

Be My Eyes

Be My Eyes was created to help people blind or with limited vision. The app is made up of a global community of people who are blind or have limited vision, along with volunteers who are visually impaired.

Be My Eyes captures the power of technology and the human connection to bring sight to people who have lost sight.

Seeing AI

Seeing AÍ is an artificial intelligence application developed by Microsoft for iOS. The app uses the cell phone's camera to identify people, objects, money, scenes, products, manuscripts, documents, colors and lighting. Then, the application describes with a voice what was identified.

It's super easy to use, it's free and available in several languages. The advantage of Seeing AÍ is its convenience, as it brings together functions from several apps in a single place. In addition, the artificial intelligence system is in constant improvement, offering more speed and efficiency in identification.

NVDA

is an open source screen reader software designed for people with visual impairment. NVDA allows blind or low vision users to access and use computers and other electronic devices independently by converting text displayed on the screen into synthesized speech aloud or into Braille, allowing them to interact with applications, browse the web, and perform other tasks.

NVDA is compatible with most Windows applications and user interfaces, and also supports web browsing and access to multimedia content. Furthermore, NVDA is highly customizable, allowing users to configure their voice preferences, speed and other settings to suit their individual needs.

iBrailler Notes

iBrailler Notes is an application for iOS that allows you to write in Braille on iPad. With it, you can type in Braille directly to create, edit and share notes. To make its use even easier, the app must be used in conjunction with the VoiceOver feature of iOS.

The program offers a unique keyboard, in which the keys are what find the user's fingers, and, in case the user doesn't feel comfortable with his keyboard, it is possible to build a new whenever you want, as many times as you want.

Pay Voice

Pay Voice installed on the cell phone allows the user to hear how much he is paying before entering the password for purchases in debit or credit.

A free cell phone application for consumers with visual impairment allows you to confirm by voice the value of purchases paid with debit or credit card machines. And you don't need to be connected to the internet.

BrailleBack

BrailleBack is an Accessibility Service that helps blind users to use braille devices. It works in conjunction with the TalkBack application to provide a combined braille and speech experience.

This application allows you to connect a compatible refreshable braille display to your device via Bluetooth. The screen content will be displayed on the screen in braille and you can navigate and interact with the device by using the keys on the screen. You can enter text using the braille keyboard.
